## Peru Agricultural Sector

### Key numbers
- 2.2 million farming families (MIDAGRI census)
- Agriculture employs ~25% of Peru's workforce
- ~70% of agricultural production comes from smallholders (<10 hectares)

### Coffee
- Peru is the #1 organic coffee exporter globally
- 223,000 coffee-producing families
- 425,000 hectares under coffee cultivation
- Key regions: Cajamarca (Jaen, San Ignacio), San Martin (Moyobamba, Rioja), Junin (Chanchamayo, Satipo), Cusco (La Convencion), Amazonas, Puno (Sandia)
- Cooperatives handle ~30% of export volume
- Junta Nacional del Cafe: 56 member cooperatives, 70,000 families

### Cacao
- Peru is a top 10 global cacao producer, top 2 in fine/flavor cacao
- ~150,000 hectares, growing
- Key regions: San Martin (Tocache, Juanjui), Cusco (VRAEM), Junin, Amazonas, Piura
- Specialty/organic segment growing rapidly

### Banano organico
- Peru is a top global organic banana exporter
- Concentrated in Piura (Sullana, Marcavelica)
- ~10,000 producers
- Strong cooperative structure (APPBOSA, APBOSMAM, CEPIBO)

### Quinoa
- Peru is a top 2 global quinoa producer
- Key region: Puno (high altitude, 3,800m+)
- COOPAIN Cabana: 575-692 producers, exports to USA, EU, China

## Financial Inclusion Data

### The gap
- **41.8%** of rural adults have financial system access (vs 65.5% urban)
- **~30%** probability of a smallholder accessing formal credit
- **76%** of rural adults lack even a deposit account
- National target: **75%** banked adult population (ENIF strategy)
- Rural financial inclusion reached **61.6%** nationally in Q2 2025 (SBS) — but this includes simple savings accounts, not credit access

### Credit barriers for smallholders
1. No formal financial history or credit score
2. No formal land titles (can't provide collateral)
3. Geographic isolation (branches can't justify rural presence)
4. Misaligned products (monthly repayments vs seasonal harvests)
5. Documentation complexity (pushes farmers to informal lenders at 30%+ interest)
6. Interest rate caps (Law 31443) make it harder to profitably serve risky borrowers, increasing need for better risk assessment

### Government response
- Agrobanco projects **166,000 clients** and **S/1.8 billion** in placements for 2026
- Agrobanco represents **31%** of all agricultural sector clients
- **S/100 million** FIFPPA fund approved to reduce interest rates from 30% to 12.44% for producers with <10 hectares (targeting 55,800 new loans)
- Government projects **112,000 agricultural credits** and **107,000+ producers** benefited by July 2026
- Seguro Agricola Catastrofico (SAC): 109,178 farmers compensated in 2023-2024

### Sources
- SBS National Financial Inclusion Policy
- Agraria.pe (agrobanco projections)
- MIDAGRI (FIFPPA fund, PPA registry)

## Financial Institutions

### State bank

**Agrobanco**
- State agricultural development bank. Present in 73% of districts.
- 127,000 credits / S/1.443 billion in 2025. Projects 166,000 clients in 2026.
- 31% of all ag sector clients. 40% of portfolio is women. 3.29% delinquency.
- Why Chacra: expanding to unbanked smallholders, needs better credit assessment.
- Web: agrobanco.com.pe

### Cajas Rurales (CRACs)

**Caja Los Andes** — Largest surviving CRAC. 227,000 savers, 85,000 borrowers, 100+ branches. Acquired Edpyme Alternativa (Feb 2025). Received $10M from Accion for digital transformation. Aspires to become Peru's first rural bank. Web: losandes.pe
**Caja Rural Prymera** — Micro/small enterprise credit. C- rating.
**Caja Rural Incasur** — Small business. C- rating. Smallest losses.
**Caja Rural del Centro** — Central highlands. Recently downgraded.

### Microfinance

**Mibanco** — Largest microfinance bank in Peru and Latin America. 70% of offices in rural areas. 28.7% microfinance market share. Web: mibanco.com.pe
**Financiera Confianza** — BBVA Microfinance Foundation. Products specifically for rural farming. Suspended crop insurance for 2025-2026 (makes credit scoring more important). Web: confianza.pe

### Edpymes

**Edpyme Alternativa** (now part of Caja Los Andes) — 91% rural micro-entrepreneurs. 6 departments, 30 agencies. Largest product: "Credito Rural." Partners: Kiva, Huruma Fund (EUR 4M). Web: alternativa.com.pe

### Cajas Municipales (CMACs)

**Caja Cusco** — Received IDB Invest US$25M specifically for agricultural portfolio growth + ethnic minorities. Web: cmac-cusco.com.pe
**Caja Piura** — Piura region (major agricultural zone). Founded 1982. Web: cajapiura.pe
**Caja Huancayo** — Junin region (highland agriculture).
**Caja Arequipa** — Most profitable CMAC. Southern agricultural corridor.

### Cooperative lenders

**COOPAC Norandino** — Savings & credit cooperative ("by farmers, for farmers"). 27,500 members, 72% rural, 33% women. 63% agricultural loan portfolio. COFIDES partnership for digital transformation ($4M+). Huruma Fund EUR 3M. Web: coopacnorandino.com

## International Impact Lenders

These lend to cooperatives and need farmer-level credit data.

**Root Capital** — Nonprofit agricultural lender. Lima office (25+ staff). 85% coffee, 11% cacao in Peru. Borrowers increase revenue 20%/year. Peru clients: Cacao de Aroma, CAPAA, COAS, Crediflorida, Cooparm, Valle Verde. Web: rootcapital.org

**Shared Interest** — UK cooperative lending society. Fair trade focus. 70% of customers have no other financial support. Peru clients: CECANOR, Mishagro, Valleinca, COOPARM, Cuencas del Huallaga, Candela. Web: shared-interest.com

**Oikocredit** — Agriculture unit launched FROM Lima in 2014. EUR 40.3M to 85 fair trade partners. Web: oikocredit.coop

**Huruma Fund** — Spain. EUR 4M to Edpyme Alternativa + EUR 3M to COOPAC Norandino. Specifically for agricultural lending. Web: fondohuruma.com

**CSAF (Council on Smallholder Agricultural Finance)** — Alliance of: Root Capital, Oikocredit, Shared Interest, Triodos, Incofin, Global Partnerships, Alterfin, Rabobank, responsAbility. $600M disbursed to 672 businesses / 2.1M farmers. One partnership = access to all members.

### Second-tier development

**COFIDE** — State development bank. Channels funds through 66 financial institutions / 1,040 service points. PRIDER program: 18,600 families in UNICAs across 9 regions. If Chacra validated by COFIDE, becomes standard across all downstream institutions. Web: cofide.com.pe

## Government Programs & Policy

### FIDA / IFAD Peru
- 44 years of partnership. Nearly USD 200M across 12 projects, 4M+ rural households.
- **Avanzar Rural (2020-2025):** USD 71.4M (IFAD $24M + Gov $41.1M). Amazonas, Ancash, Cajamarca, Lima, San Martin. 20,178 families (exceeded 17,400 target). Named IFAD's best-performing active project globally in 2024 (out of 190+). Income: S/1,574 to S/2,015/month. Financial inclusion: 23% to 64% formal savings. 102 FOREs, 6,371 loans.
- **MERCAGRO (2026, newly approved):** USD 44.6M (IFAD $26M). 75,332 producers in 77 districts across Apurimac, Ayacucho, Huancavelica, Huanuco, Junin, Pasco, Puno, Ucayali. Focus: market access, commercialization, inclusive value chains.
- Country Director: **Juan Diego Ruiz Complido**
- Web: ifad.org/en/w/countries/peru

### MIDAGRI (Ministerio de Desarrollo Agrario)
- Budget S/3,369M for 2025 (+19.6% YoY). Three priorities: small producers, food availability, investment.
- **AgroDigital:** Mobile app for 400,000+ coffee/cacao farmers. Launched in Tarapoto with EU, CAF, BID, World Bank. Digital skills training in indigenous languages.
- **PPA (Padron de Productores Agrarios):** Digital identity for 2,030,000 farmers (38 data points each). Georeference, damage reporting, EUDR compliance.
- **SIEA-BI:** Satellite frost analysis, agroclimate modeling, "AgroChatea" for real-time price queries.
- **FAE-AGRO:** Agricultural credit facilitation through financial institutions including COOPACs.
- FIFPPA fund: S/100M for reduced-interest agricultural credit.
- Web: gob.pe/midagri

### Sierra y Selva Exportadora (now "Agromercado")
- Renamed by Congress. Autonomous budget. Expanded competencies.
- 2023: 25,532 producers commercialized S/188M through SSE. 27 Commercial Articulation Plans generating S/26M for selva producers.
- Focus: coffee, cacao, quinoa, superfoods, native products.
- Functions: financial instruments for productive chains, market linkage, technology transfer.
- Web: gob.pe/sse

- FIDA/IFAD-funded. See FIDA section above for full data.

- 120 UNICAs formed, 1,812 producers, S/2.77M social capital, 7,604 loans.

## Regulatory Landscape

### EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR)
- Effective 2025
- Requires per-farm traceability for coffee, cacao, soy, palm oil imports
- Peruvian cooperatives exporting to Europe MUST have auditable farmer-level data
- Creates urgent demand for exactly what Chacra produces

### SBS Financial Inclusion (ENIF)
- Multi-sectoral policy: MEF + SBS + MIDAGRI
- Target: 75% banked adults (from 61.6% national, 41.8% rural)
- Alternative credit scoring for unbanked populations explicitly encouraged

### Ley 29733 (Data Protection)
- Peru's personal data protection law
- Requires consent for data processing
- Chacra must: get explicit consent at registration, allow data deletion, anonymize credit profiles

### Interest Rate Caps (Law 31443)
- Limits interest rates on microloans
- Makes it harder to serve risky (undocumented) borrowers profitably
- Increases need for better risk assessment tools (Chacra's credit scoring)

## Competitive Landscape

### Closest competitor: Agros

**Agros** — Peruvian agritech startup. Blockchain-based digital identities for smallholder farmers.
- **How it works:** Field agents ("Inges") collect soil samples, georeference plots. Digital identity enables credit scoring by financieras (partner: Caja Sullana), input purchases (Netafim Peru), precision ag, telemedicine.
- **Traction:** 10,000+ farmers across Peru. 1,000 digitized clients, 50+ credits, 30 soil studies, 300 input sales, 10 local agents.
- **Funded by:** ProInnovate (Reto Bio).
- **International:** Through Agridigitalizacion project (IICA + Agriterra + IFAD), now 10,500+ farmers across Peru, Bolivia, Guatemala, Haiti, Honduras.
- **CEO:** Robinson Lopez Monzon. Co-founder: Hugo Pinarreta.
- **Key difference from Chacra:** Agros focuses on individual farmer digital identity + blockchain + field agents. Chacra focuses on cooperative-level traceability, offline-first data capture by the FARMER (not an agent), and credit scoring from transaction data. Potentially complementary.
- LinkedIn: agrosoficial

### Peru traceability platforms

**COPERAWEB** — ERP for traceability, based in Tarapoto, San Martin. Connects scales/roasters to data platform. Targets export cooperatives. Web: coperaweb.com
**Agritracer** — Farm management with offline app for field workers. Harvest, post-harvest, phytosanitary traceability. Web: agritracer.pe
**TrackitAgro** — Harvest and post-harvest traceability monitoring.
**Agro Nativo Digital** — ESAN-researched concept for coffee cooperative traceability in San Martin.

### Key insight
The traceability market in Peru is fragmented, export-focused (EUDR compliance), and mostly online-dependent. None combine offline-first farmer-operated input with credit scoring output.

### Adjacent solutions (global)

| Solution | What | Limitation |
|----------|------|-----------|
| Paper notebooks | Universal, zero tech | No aggregation, no credit output, gets lost |
| Cooperative Excel | Common, free | Manual, stale, no farmer input, no integrity checks |
| SAP/ERP | Enterprise ag management | Too expensive, too complex, not farmer-facing |
| FarmForce (Syngenta) | Smallholder data collection | Requires connectivity, agent-operated (not farmer) |
| Agritask | Precision agriculture platform | Large commercial farms, not smallholder, expensive |
| Cropio | Satellite + field data | Same: large-scale, not offline, not credit-oriented |
| mAgri apps (GSMA ecosystem) | Various mobile ag services | Fragmented, no credit scoring integration |
